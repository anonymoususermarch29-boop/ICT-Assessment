History of Formula 1

Formula 1 began in 1950 with the first World Championship race at Silverstone, England. In the 1950s, drivers like Juan Manuel Fangio became famous, winning five titles. The 1960s saw British teams such as Lotus rise, with Jim Clark leading the way and rear engine cars changing the sport. In the 1970s, safety rules improved after many accidents, and stars like Niki Lauda made their mark. The 1980s brought turbo engines and the famous rivalry between Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost. In the 1990s, Michael Schumacher and Ferrari started their era of success. The 2000s introduced new aerodynamics and more races around the world. In the 2010s, hybrid engines arrived, and Lewis Hamilton with Mercedes dominated. Today, Formula 1 continues to grow, with new tracks and young drivers like Max Verstappen shaping the modern era.

Signs/Flags Explained

Sign/Flag Representation Sign/Flag Name Sign/Flag Meaning
🟩 Green Flag There are no more track hazards or roadblocks, you can return to normal speed and overtaking is now allowed.
🟨 Yellow Flag Track Hazard Ahead. Immediately Slow Down and do not Overtake.
🟨🟨 Double Yellow Flag Significant Danger On Track. Slow Down, Do not Overtake and be ready to stop or pit.
🟥 Red Flag Session Suspended, Return to Pits. Major Incident or Bad Weather.
🏁 Chequered Flag There is a victory, and the session has come to an end.
🟦 Blue Flag Faster Vehicle Coming from Rear, Must Let Pass. Failure to let pass after crossing 3 consecutive blue flag posts will result in penalty.
⚪️ White Flag Slow Vehicle Ahead.(e.g.:Ambulance, Recovery Vehicle)
🟨🟥 Yellow/Red Flag Track Ahead is Slippery and has poor Track Grip. Due to Water, Oil or Debris
⚫️ [Driver Number] Black Flag Immediate Disqualification. Driver is to return to pits immediately.
⚫️🟠 [Driver Number] Orange Disc Black Flag Detected Mechanic Failure from driver. Damaged car must pit to check and fix if possible immediately.
⚪⚫ Slanted Black White Flag Unsportmanlike Behaviour Warning to Driver.
VSC Virtual Safety Car Slow down and don't overtake, track hazard or track sweep but not huge and close to pit stop. Drivers must follow a delta time.
SC Safety Car Brought out in more extreme cases. Do not overtake, slow down and follow the lead of safety car bunch. Only unfair case of overtake is when driver is 1 or more laps behind leader and is permitted to pass safety car to unlap before Safety Car is cancelled.
🟪6️⃣0️⃣ Code 60 Faster and Safe Alternative for all Vehicles to slow down. Overtaking or crossing 60 km/h is strictly forbidden.
